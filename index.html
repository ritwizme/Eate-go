Idli

A savory cake of south Indian origin popular throughout India. The cakes are about two to three inches in diameter and are made by steaming a batter consisting of fermented black lentils (de-husked) and rice or semolina (rava). The fermentation process breaks down the starches so that they are easily metabolized by the body.
Mostly eaten for breakfast or as a snack, idlys are served with three types of chutneys and sambar.


Fun Facts

Idli (and the process of steaming) was known in India as early as 700 CE. The process of steaming was influenced from Indonesia subsequently between 800-1200 CE, giving rise to the modern day Idli.

Dosa

Dosa is a fermented crepe or pancake made from rice batter and black lentils. It is a common breakfast dish and street food in south India. It is rich in carbohydrates, contains no sugar or saturated fats, is gluten-free and contains protein.
